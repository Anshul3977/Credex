# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User
**Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series-A startup (10–50 people),
using 3+ AI tools, paying $500–$5,000/month in AI subscriptions,
with no centralized way to track or benchmark that spend.**

They feel the bill but can't justify it to the board. They signed up 
for tools during a hackathon and never audited since.

## What They Google Before Wanting This
- "how much does github copilot cost for a team"
- "cursor vs copilot pricing 2025"
- "reduce AI tool costs startup"
- "ChatGPT team vs enterprise which plan"
- "best AI tools for small engineering team"

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

1. **Post on Hacker News Show HN** on a Tuesday morning 9am ET:
   "Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend"
   One genuine post with a real savings example as the lede.

2. **Post in r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups** — not as an ad,
   as a question: "We built a free AI spend auditor — would this 
   be useful for your team? Here's what we found for a 15-person startup."

3. **Cold DM 50 CTOs on LinkedIn** with a personalized note:
   "Hey [name], saw you're using Cursor + Copilot — we built a free 
   2-min audit that shows if you're on the right plan. 
   No signup required. Happy to share."

4. **Tweet the results page** (not the homepage) — share a real 
   audit result showing $X savings with the shareable URL. 
   The OG image does the selling.

5. **Post in Indie Hackers** as a build log — "How I built a 
   free AI spend auditor in 7 days" with a link.

## Unfair Distribution Channel
Credex already talks to companies buying discounted AI credits.
Every inbound Credex lead is a warm audience for SpendLens.
Reverse the funnel: SpendLens generates leads → Credex closes them.
No competitor has this because no competitor is Credex.
