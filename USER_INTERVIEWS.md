# User Interviews

## Attempted Outreach

I sent cold DMs to 6 people across LinkedIn and X/Twitter between 
May 7–12 asking for 10-minute conversations about AI tool spend. 
Three did not respond within the submission window. Two declined. 
One conversation happened informally (see below).

I'm documenting this honestly rather than fabricating interviews. 
The assignment explicitly flags fabricated interviews as an instant 
reject. I'd rather lose points for incomplete outreach than lose 
the submission for dishonesty.

What I would have asked:
- What AI tools does your team currently pay for?
- Do you know your monthly AI tool spend off the top of your head?
- Have you ever audited or benchmarked it against alternatives?
- What would make you trust a free tool that showed you savings?
- Would you share a results page on Twitter if it showed real savings?

## Informal Conversation — A.K., Developer, Early-stage startup

Not a formal interview — a 10-minute Telegram conversation with a 
friend who works at a 4-person startup.

**Role:** Full-stack developer
**Company stage:** Pre-seed, 4 people

**What he said (paraphrased from notes):**
- "We pay for Cursor Pro for 3 people and ChatGPT Plus individually. 
  I have no idea what the total is."
- "I signed up for Copilot during a GitHub promo and forgot to cancel. 
  Still paying for it."
- "I wouldn't trust a tool like this unless the pricing data was 
  clearly sourced. I'd check one number manually before believing 
  the rest."

**Most surprising thing:** He had a redundant Copilot subscription 
he'd forgotten about entirely. This validated the redundancy 
detection rule in the audit engine.

**What it changed:** Added a note on the results page that says 
"Sources: official pricing pages, verified May 2026" to build 
credibility. Also made the reasoning text on each card more specific 
rather than generic.

## What I'd Do Differently

If I had started outreach on Day 1 instead of Day 5, I would have 
had 3 real interviews. The lesson: user research has a lead time 
that code doesn't. You can write code at 2am. You can't interview 
someone at 2am.

For Round 2, I'd run these interviews in the first 48 hours and 
let the findings shape the build rather than validate it after.
